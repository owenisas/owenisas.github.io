Understanding Income Inequality

Income inequality is a major social issue in the United States, affecting millions of people across different demographics. The gap between the wealthy and the poor continues to widen due to systemic structures, discriminatory policies, and economic changes. Factors like the gender pay gap, racial disparities, and unequal access to education and healthcare contribute to this growing divide. From a sociological perspective, income inequality is not just about money—it reflects deeper issues within society, including class struggles, institutional discrimination, and policy failures. This website explores the causes, consequences, and potential solutions to income inequality through a sociological lens.
